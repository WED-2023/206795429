    About Him

    Both Grandma and Grandpa, my father's parents, immigrated to Israel from Iraq in 1951. They married
    soon after meeting each other. My father was born in 1959 and had a modest and joyful childhood. He
    attended "Beit Yaacov" school in Rehovot and also helped at home as best he could. He often accompanied
    my Grandpa to his job and assisted him there, as well as helping my Grandma sell items crafted by her.

    After graduating, he served in the Israeli Air Force as an airplane technician. Following his military
    service, he began working at "Tenuva" dairy in Rehovot. He married my mom Rachel in 1991, and my older
    sister was born soon after in 1992. We are three sisters in total. My older sister got married and gave
    birth to my beautiful niece, whom my father loved more than ever.

    In September 2021, my father was diagnosed with cancer, and after a prolonged battle with the disease,
    he passed away last December. My father was a kind, smart, and generous man.
    His memory will always be with us.

    Q1. Here are some main differnces between web1, web2 and web3:
    Level of control and authority
    Web1: Few big companies control everything.
    Web2: Users can participate more but still rely on big platforms like Facebook.
    Web3: No big boss; everyone shares control using new tech like blockchain.

    What Can User Do
    Web1: You mostly just read stuff.
    Web2: You can post, share, and chat.
    Web3: You're in charge. You create content, make decisions, and own your data.

    Who Owns Users Data
    Web1: Companies own your data.
    Web2: You have a bit more control, but companies still have a say.
    Web3: You own your data and control who can use it.


    Q2. Here is an example of a use case showing the differences between web1, web2 and web3:
    The use case: searching for a cake recipe across Web1, Web2, and Web3:
    On Web1: You find a static website with a single recipe owned by the website creator.
    You can only read the recipe but can't interact with it or share your own.
    On Web2: You discover a cooking blog where a blogger shares recipes and tips.
    You can read the recipe, interact with other users' comments, and share the recipe on social media.
    You have more interaction and sharing options compared to Web1.
    On Web3: You come across a decentralized recipe-sharing platform on the blockchain.
    Recipes are contributed by users globally, and everyone can add their own recipes or variations.
    Each recipe is protected by a smart contract, ensuring fair credit to the creator. You have full
    control over your data and recipes, and the community verifies the authenticity of recipes.
